 ## 2.Experiential Perspective Prompt:
 An Experiential Perspective Prompt is a type of prompt that asks you to respond based on your personal experience, role, or viewpoint in a given situation. Instead of only analyzing facts, you explain how you (or someone in a role) would feel, act, or decide in that scenario.
 ### example:
  Imagine you are a production manager in a manufacturing company. How would you feel about replacing manual monitoring with IoT-based automation, and what challenges would you expect?
  As an engineer working on implementing embedded controllers in manufacturing equipment, describe your experience in integrating them for predictive maintenance.
  From the perspective of a factory worker, how would automation and predictive maintenance impact your daily work routine and job satisfaction?

 ## 3.Everyday Functioning prompt:
   An Everyday Functioning Prompt is a type of prompt that asks you to relate a concept or situation to daily life or practical routine activities. It helps to connect technical or abstract ideas with real-world, everyday functioning.
   ### example:
   How is the use of IoT in manufacturing similar to how smart home devices (like smart thermostats or smart plugs) improve daily energy efficiency?
   Relate predictive maintenance in manufacturing to everyday situations, such as servicing your bike or car before it breaks down.
   How does automating production equipment compare to using household appliances like washing machines or dishwashers that run automatically without constant human supervision?

   Act as a factory manager and explain to your workers why automation with embedded controllers is important.

## 1.comparative analysis prompt:
 A comparative analysis prompt is an instruction that asks you to perform this type of comparison. It typically identifies the subjects to be analyzed and often provides specific criteria or a framework for the comparison. A good prompt is clear and directs you toward a meaningful analysis beyond simply listing similarities and differences.
 ### example:
   Compare a traditional, manually monitored manufacturing process with the proposed IoT-driven automated system. Your analysis should focus on key performance indicators such as efficiency, operational costs, energy consumption, and maintenance schedules.

 ## 2. Experiential Perspective Prompt:
 An experiential perspective prompt asks you to analyze a situation or topic from the viewpoint of someone directly involved in it. Unlike a traditional analysis that uses an objective, third-person voice, this prompt requires you to step into a specific role and explain how that experience shapes your understanding, decisions, or feelings. The goal is to demonstrate empathy and a nuanced, first-person understanding of a particular viewpoint. It moves beyond simply describing a situation to embodying a role within it.
 ### example:
 You are a seasoned factory floor supervisor with 25 years of experience in manual monitoring. From your perspective, describe your initial skepticism and eventual adaptation to the new IoT-driven automation system. Discuss the challenges you faced in training your team and the impact the change had on your daily responsibilities and job security.

 ## 3.Everyday Functioning Prompt:
 An everyday functioning prompt asks you to describe and analyze how a system, process, or technology operates on a routine, day-to-day basis. It focuses on the practical, ground-level application rather than high-level strategy or a single, major event. This type of prompt is designed to explore the tangible impacts, challenges, and benefits that are part of the normal course of operation. The goal is to understand how something works in practice, from the perspective of a regular user or operator.
 ### example:
 The manufacturing industry is looking to reduce manual monitoring and increase efficiency through automation. The system will utilize IoT devices and embedded controllers to automate equipment, monitor performance, and enable predictive maintenance. The goal is to streamline the production process, minimize downtime, and enhance energy efficiency.

 ## 4.Universal Prompt Structures Prompt Refinements:
 Definition: Universal Prompt Structures Prompt Refinements
Universal Prompt Structures Prompt Refinements refers to the practice of taking a broad, generic prompt (a "universal prompt") and making it more specific and effective for a particular context or scenario. This involves adding details, constraints, and a clear focus to a simple command. The goal is to transform a vague request into a well-defined task that elicits a more precise, high-quality, and useful response. It's about turning an open-ended question into a targeted one.
### example:

Critique: This is too broad. It could lead to a very high-level or overly technical explanation. It lacks a specific audience or purpose.

## 5.Prompt Size Limitations design an AI 
Prompt Size Limitations Design refers to the strategic process of creating an AI system that can handle and effectively respond to prompts of varying lengths, especially when a platform or model has a strict character or token limit. This design principle is crucial for building practical and resilient AI applications. It involves anticipating that users will provide both very short and very long prompts, and designing the system to either gracefully handle the limitations (e.g., by truncating the input or asking for clarification) or to operate efficiently within them. The goal is to maximize the usefulness of the AI despite the constraints on the user's input size.
### example:
User Prompt: Explain the new system."
